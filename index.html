About Me

I am a senior at Depaul University studying to get my Bachelor of Science, majoring in Software Development and minoring in Game Design. I used to work as a Cybernavigator at the Chicago Public Library, providing patrons with curated computer classes and helping them breach the digital divide in underprivileged communities. I also help patrons with hardware and software issues they might be having with their devices. Currently I work for Midwest Jesuits where I am interning as an IT Operator. This internship has me doing things similar to my previous job, but at a more technical scale. I've also been able to implement a ticket system at this opffice to help streamline helping employees in and out of the office. Our current project entails us moving our on-premise servers to the cloud. This has given me a great deal of networking experience and im excited to see what else I can learn.

My academic journey has given me a broad and robust foundation, teaching me how to develop code as well as express myself creatively through games. I have learned about the principles of game design, how to create characters and stories, and how to use code to bring these elements to life. I have also learned about the business side of game development, such as how to market and sell games. My academic journey has prepared me for a career in game development, and I am excited to use my skills to create games that people will enjoy.

My Projects

During my Junior year I took a class called IoT Netowrking (CSE321) where we developed a robot capable of manuevering a room using ultrasound radars. We used the radars to detect surfaces infront of the robot. When the distance of the surface hit a certain threshold, it would backup for 1 second, turn left, and continue its journey. Using this simple process its able to manuver classrooms with ease.

During my Senior year I took a class called Object-Oriented Software Development (SE350) where I developed a small scale simulation for stock markets. You input how many trades you want done, and it'll auto-generate stocks and simulate a market.
